GIRAFFE

The giraffe is the tallest land animal, known for its long neck and legs. Native to Africa, it inhabits savannas, grasslands, and open woodlands. Giraffes feed mainly on leaves from acacia trees, using their long tongues to reach high branches. Their unique spotted patterns help with camouflage. Giraffes are social animals, often seen in loose groups called towers. Despite their size, they can run up to 35 miles per hour over short distances.
