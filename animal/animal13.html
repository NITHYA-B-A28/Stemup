ZEBRA

The zebra is a wild animal known for its distinctive black and white stripes. It belongs to the horse family and lives mainly in the grasslands and savannas of Africa. Each zebra has a unique stripe pattern, much like a fingerprint. Zebras are herbivores and graze on grasses, leaves, and bark. They live in groups called herds for safety from predators like lions. Zebras are fast runners and use their speed and strong kicks to escape danger. Their stripes may help confuse predators and keep them cool under the hot sun.
