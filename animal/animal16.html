TORTOISE

The tortoise is a slow-moving reptile known for its hard, dome-shaped shell that protects its body. Unlike turtles, tortoises live on land and cannot swim. They are herbivores, feeding on grasses, fruits, and leafy plants. Tortoises have long lifespans, with some living over 100 years. They move slowly but steadily, and their strong legs help them walk on rough ground. Tortoises are peaceful animals and often live alone. Their calm nature and long life make them symbols of patience and wisdom in many cultures.
