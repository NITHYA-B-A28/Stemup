ELEPHANT

The elephant is the largest land animal on Earth, known for its massive size and intelligence. It has a long trunk used for breathing, smelling, grabbing objects, and drinking water. Elephants are social animals that live in herds, usually led by a matriarch. They are herbivores, feeding on grass, leaves, bark, and fruit. Elephants are found mainly in Africa and Asia, with African elephants being larger. Their tusks, made of ivory, are often targeted by poachers, leading to conservation concerns. Elephants are gentle giants and play a vital role in their ecosystems.
