FLAMINGO

The flamingo is a tall, elegant bird known for its bright pink feathers and long, slender legs. It lives near lakes, lagoons, and wetlands in parts of Africa, Asia, the Americas, and Europe. Flamingos get their pink color from the algae and shrimp they eat. They are social birds and live in large colonies for protection and mating. Flamingos often stand on one leg, which helps them conserve body heat. They use their curved beaks to filter food from water. Their graceful movements and striking appearance make them truly unique among birds.
