FISH

Fish are aquatic animals that live in both freshwater and saltwater environments like rivers, lakes, and oceans. They breathe through gills and use fins to swim. Fish come in a wide variety of shapes, sizes, and colors, with species ranging from tiny goldfish to giant sharks. Most fish lay eggs, and many travel in groups called schools. They are cold-blooded and have a streamlined body to help them move easily in water. Fish are an important part of the food chain and a vital source of food for humans and other animals.
